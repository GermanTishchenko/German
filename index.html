Five Nights of Iron

In an effort to better understand the game engine of Hearts of Iron 4 I've challenged myself to recreate horror game Five Nights at Freddy's in HOI4 engine

The project was conducted solely by me in 3 months with some outside help with voice acting. The mod had gained over 50 000 views on Steam Workshop page, but unfortunately it was taken down for unknown reasons

Logo of the modification

Overview

Five Nights of Iron was an modification for the game Hearts of Iron 4 conducted in Clausewitz engine and available through Steam Workshop. Hearts of Iron 4 is a grand strategy game set in World War 2 where you have to play as one of the countries militarily defeat the enemy side. My job consisted of modifying the existing scripts and visual elements, as well as completely overhauling game mechanics to turn it into a first-person horror game

Challenges

Modifying pre-existing code was more challenging than making a game from scratch, as I was limited by what I was able to do by the tools I had in my disposal. Hearts of Iron 4 operates using scripts that are made by developers and shared to the modders, as such my toolkit was fairly limited and I had to come up with innovative solutions. For example it was impossible for me to remove the "Pause" function, so I made the "Unpause" button cover the entire screen so it would be impossible to play unpaused.

Content

Apart from the code I had to completely overhaul the visual aspect of the game. I used Photoshop and the game Garry's Mod to create assets and the scenery of the game. Below is the office of the game, which was made with different pictures taken from the internet and modified in Photoshop to fit in the mod. It uses a combination of random stock photos with lowered contrast and low poly objects from other games

In-game office of the mod

Testing

The game was tested multiple times in order to find any bugs or problems with balance. The game Hearts of Iron 4 provides an error document, which I used to find problems with the code. I also received feedback from the players and made occasional fixes with new patches

Conclusion

In conclusion I have to say that I didn't expect this project to receive as much attention as it did. Most of it came from the attachment to the already existing IP's (Hearts of Iron, Five Nights at Freddy's) and the unique gameplay I was able to provide.
